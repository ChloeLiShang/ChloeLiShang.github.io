I'm a student at the University of Alberta majoring in Computing Science and soon to graduate in June.

I have participated in two hackathon events in my third year. The first time was just to see what a hackathon is and the second time I actually made an app with my friends that I am proud of (you can check it out here).

Last semester I built a sports event recording web app (Measure What Matters) for my CMPUT401 class with four other students. I worked on the front-end of the app. This project gave me experience like I was actually in a working environment. We practiced agile methodology and had an actual client so the app is designed based on their requirements.

I just made a social distribution website with other students for a school project. I was in charge of the front end of this project and also designed some object models. We built the website using Django and hosted it with heroku app.

In my third year, I made an android Uber-like app with other students. I was responsible for the UI design and the implementation on the rider side. And additional stuff like solving merge conflicts and fixing bugs. Check out the project on GitHub!
